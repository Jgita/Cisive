Frequently Asked Questions

What photos are required for the windshield damage inspection?

One photo is required of the full windshield view showing the damage. If the damage is not visible, a close-up photo(s) of the windshield damage is required to complete the inspection.

Additionally, one photo is required of a close-up of the VIN plate on the dashboard. The VIN plate can be found by looking at the dashboard on the driver’s side of the vehicle.

A maximum of ‘3’ photos may be selected for the front full windshield view and the VIN plate on dashboard, and a maximum of ‘6’ close-up windshield damage photo(s) if needed.

Where can I find my vehicle’s VIN plate on the dashboard?

The VIN plate can be found by looking at the dashboard through the windshield on the driver’s side of the vehicle.

Capturing windshield damage inspection photo(s)

Position your camera in the Landscape (horizontal) position to capture the photo(s) of the front full windshield view and close-up windshield damage. You may need to position yourself several feet away from the front of the vehicle in order to capture the full front windshield view in the photo. What type of windshield damage should I take a picture of?

Any and all damage seen on the windshield MUST be photographed and recorded in the “Vehicle Condition” section. Damages include but are not limited to:

  • Chips
  • Cracks
  • Star Cracks
  • Pits
  • Smashed Windshields

For every damage photo taken, the corresponding damage must be selected or checked in the “Vehicle Conditions” section. For example: if you select “CENTER, CRACKED” damage, you must first have provided a separate photo of the crack.

*Please note: you will not be able to add more than SIX (6) damage photo(s) to your inspection.

The photo(s) are not dragging/dropping or uploading to the select photo(s) page.

Be sure that the photo files are in JPEG or PNG format. PDF files are not compatible with this webpage.

Navigating to the next page.

Once all required fields are completed on the inspection entry screens, you can navigate to the subsequent page. The required fields are noted with red text as REQUIRED.

How may I acquire a copy of my windshield inspection?

At the conclusion of the inspection, you will be emailed a copy of your report. The inspection will be emailed to the address provided in your profile. You may also view the inspection directly on your desktop.
