Frequently Asked Questions

Capturing Inspection Photo(s)

How do I capture clear photo(s) of my vehicle?

Position your smart-phone or tablet in the Landscape (Horizontal) position; then, tap your screen to focus on vehicle and capture the photo(s). A CARCO pre-insurance inspection must include at LEAST the following three photographs: FRONT & DRIVER’S SIDE, REAR & PASSENGER SIDE, and EPA STICKER. See example photo(s) displayed below:

1) FRONT & DRIVER’S SIDE
2) REAR & PASSENGER SIDE
3) EPA STICKER

The FRONT & DRIVER’S SIDE should include the entire driver side of the vehicle (bumper to bumper) showing entire front bumper.

The REAR & PASSENGER SIDE should include the entire passenger side (bumper to bumper) showing entire rear bumper.

If you are submitting an inspection for a vehicle registered in the state of Massachusetts, you will be required to take the following vehicle photo angles:

1) FRONT & PASSENGER SIDE
2) REAR & DRIVER’S SIDE

The FRONT & PASSENGER SIDE should include the entire passenger side (bumper to bumper) showing entire front bumper. The READ & DRIVER’S SIDE should include the entire driver’s side (bumper to bumper) showing entire rear bumper.

The EPA STICKER, usually located on the driver’s side doorjamb, will feature the VIN number, as well as additional factory information. If the EPA sticker is missing entirely, you must STILL provide a photo of the blank space on the doorjamb. Also be sure to check the “EPA Sticker is Missing” box on the Select photo(s) screen.

Identifying the EPA sticker on your car

What is the EPA sticker?

Contains vehicle information such as vehicle manufacturer, vehicle weight and includes the 17 digit Vehicle Identification Number (VIN). Ensure that the VIN is clearly visible when capturing the picture of the sticker.

Where is the EPA sticker located on my car?

The EPA sticker is located on the driver's side door or doorjamb.

I cannot locate EPA sticker on my car, what should I do?

Capture the picture of driver's side doorjamb where there sticker would have been located. Also, be sure to check "EPA Sticker is Missing" box on Upload photo(s) screen.

How do I capture a clear photo of the EPA Sticker on my car?

Based on the position of the sticker on your vehicle, position your camera in the horizontal or vertical position to align with the EPA then tap the screen to focus on sticker and capture photo.

Insured Information

After capturing the inspection photo(s), you will need to enter your information including your insurance company name, policy # (if available), name (last, first), and full address.

Vehicle Information, Options and Accessories

You will need to enter your vehicle description information, including but not limited to; the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN), year, model and make, options and accessories.

Vehicle Physical Condition/Damage

You will need to record any and all existing damage on your vehicle.

Capturing Damage Photo(s)

What type of vehicle damage should I take photo(s) of?

Any and all damage(s) seen on the vehicle MUST be photographed and recorded in the "Vehicle Condition" section. Exterior & Interior damages, including but are not limited to: dents, dings, scratches, rust, missing hubcaps, broken/missing sections of vehicle body, glass damage, worn/torn seats, console/sound system and dashboard.

For every section of Damage that is checked in the "Vehicle Condition" section, a corresponding damage photo MUST be taken and added to the inspection. For example: if you select “FRONT BUMPER” damage, you must first provide a separate photo of the damaged bumper.

*Please note: you will not be able to add more than FIFTEEN (15) damage photo(s) to your inspection.
